A Legend Born From War

Torpedoed in 1942.
Yours to Explore Today.

January 11, 1942 — a Japanese torpedo strikes the USAT Liberty in the Lombok Strait. The crippled ship is beached on Tulamben's black sand shore, where she rests for 20 years. Then in 1963, Mount Agung erupts — tremors push her into the sea, and a legend is born. Today she lies at 3 to 30 metres, her coral-covered hull teeming with 400+ species. One of the most accessible wreck dives on the planet — and it's right here.

01 / 03

USAT Liberty
Wreck

Depth 3–30m All Levels Night Dive

The soul of Tulamben. At roughly 120 metres long, the Liberty lies so close to shore that you simply wade in from the black sand beach — making it one of the most accessible wreck dives in the world. The hull stretches from 5 to 30 metres, offering something for every level from OWD beginners to seasoned tech divers. Giant groupers, bigeye trevally schools and barracuda patrol this steel forest day and night.

  • Shore dive — no boat needed
  • Shallowest point ~3 metres
  • Outstanding night diving
  • Resident Napoleon wrasse
  • Bow propeller still visible
  • Extremely high coral coverage
  • Mild current, vis 10–25m
  • Diveable year-round
Dramatic Wall
02 / 03

Tulamben
Drop Off

Depth 5–60m Intermediate+ Current

Located about 100 metres north of the Liberty wreck, the Tulamben Drop Off is a sheer vertical wall plunging from 5 metres into the abyss. The face is smothered in soft corals, sea fans and sponges hiding all manner of tiny creatures. On the plateau above, huge parrotfish and sea turtles are regular visitors — and with luck, a manta ray may glide up silently from the deep.

  • Shore dive, black sand entry
  • Lush soft coral wall
  • Bumphead parrotfish common
  • Occasional manta ray
  • Cooler at depth ~24°C
  • AOW certification advised
  • Sunrise light is magical
  • Variable current — stay alert
Beginner Friendly
03 / 03

Coral Garden

Depth 5–18m Beginners Macro Paradise

Just south of the Liberty wreck lies a vivid shallow coral garden — the perfect playground for underwater photographers and newly certified OWD divers. Coral diversity here is exceptional: dozens of nudibranch species, pygmy seahorses and ghost pipefish hide among the rubble on every dive, turning every descent into a treasure hunt.

  • Shallow, great for beginners
  • Pygmy seahorses frequent
  • Dozens of nudibranch species
  • Best visibility in the morning
  • First choice for macro photography
  • Almost no current
  • Ghost pipefish & lionfish
  • Easily combined with the wreck

No experience is needed. The Liberty wreck starts at just 3 metres depth, making it one of the most beginner-friendly wreck dives in the world. Complete beginners can join our Intro Dive (Discover Scuba) and explore the shallow sections with a dedicated guide. PADI Open Water certified divers can access the full wreck down to 18 metres, while Advanced divers can reach the deepest sections at 30 metres.
Tulamben is approximately 2.5 hours by car from Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar. The drive takes you along the northeastern coast past Mount Agung — one of Bali's most scenic routes. We recommend hiring a private driver (around USD 30–40 one-way) or joining a shared shuttle. We are happy to recommend trusted drivers — just ask us on WhatsApp when you book.
Each site has its strengths — but Tulamben offers something unique: the USAT Liberty wreck, accessible as a shore dive with no boat required. Nusa Penida is famous for mola-mola and manta rays but requires a boat and can have strong currents. Amed is quieter and great for macro. Tulamben stands out for beginner accessibility, the iconic wreck, consistently calm conditions, and extraordinary marine biodiversity — it is widely considered one of the best shore dives in all of Asia.
All our dive packages include: full scuba equipment (BCD, regulator, wetsuit, mask, fins, tank), a PADI-certified dive guide, underwater photos and video of your dive, drinking water, and a post-dive freshwater rinse. There are no hidden fees. For course packages, all learning materials and certification fees are also included. Simply bring your swimwear, sunscreen, and a sense of adventure.
Tulamben is diveable year-round thanks to its calm, sheltered bay. The dry season (April to November) offers the best visibility — often exceeding 25–30 metres — and the calmest surface conditions. The wet season (December to March) brings occasional rain but diving conditions remain good and the site is far less crowded. Water temperature stays between 26–29°C throughout the year, so no thick wetsuit is needed.
The PADI Open Water Diver course typically takes 3 to 4 days at Paradise Dive Tulamben. Day 1 covers online theory (which you can complete before arrival) and confined water skills in our pool. Days 2–3 involve 4 open-water training dives directly on the Liberty Wreck and Coral Garden — some of the world's most beautiful classroom dives. You leave fully certified and ready to dive anywhere in the world to 18 metres.
Yes — Tulamben is one of Bali's best night diving locations. The Liberty Wreck transforms completely after dark: bioluminescent plankton, hunting lionfish, sleeping parrotfish, and octopus emerging from the coral make every night dive a completely different experience from the day. All night dives are led by an experienced guide with full lighting equipment, and the entry point is the same gentle beach slope used during the day.

  • 4 Open Water Dives — Head out to Tulamben's best dive sites to review and perfect your skills while exploring the fascinating marine environment.

Enriched Air Diver (EANx)
Rp 3.000.000 · PADI Certified
Course Description
The PADI Enriched Air Diver course is the most popular PADI specialty course. Diving with enriched air (nitrox) can increase your bottom time, especially on repetitive dives, and help you feel more refreshed after your dives. In this course you'll learn how to safely plan and conduct dives using enriched air mixtures of up to 40% oxygen.
What You'll Cover
  • What enriched air is and how it benefits divers
  • Oxygen toxicity — risks and how to manage them
  • Planning dives with enriched air using dive tables or a computer
  • Analyzing your cylinder to confirm the oxygen percentage
  • Equipment considerations for enriched air diving
Requirements
Must hold a PADI Open Water Diver Certification and be at least 10 years old. No prior enriched air experience required.
